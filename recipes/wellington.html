Weeknight Wellington

Description

classic Beef Wellington with a twist using mushroom-based spice

Ingredients

  • 5oz Button Mushrooms, coarsely chopped
  • 12oz Portobello Mushrooms, coarsely chopped
  • 1 lb 90% lean ground beef
  • 2 Tbs panko bread crumbs
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp dried thyme, divided
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/8 tsp ground black pepper
  • 4 Tbs butter
  • 2 Shallots
  • 2 Tbs Sherry

Steps

  1. Use food processor on mushrooms until finely ground. Place 5oz in large bowl. Put aside 12oz for mushroom duxelles
  2. Add ground beef, panko, egg, Worcestershire sauce, 1/2 tsp thyme, 1/4 tsp salt, and 1/8 tsp pepper to bowl with mushrooms
  3. Mix bowl til combined, place onto wax paper and shape 5x10-inch loaf
  4. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F, line baking sheet with aluminum foil
  5. Place 2 shallots into food processor and pulse til finely chopped
  6. melt butter on skillet over med-high heat add 12oz mushrooms, minced shallots and remaining thyme. Cook and stir, until mushrooms release liquid
  7. Add sherry and cook til liquid evaporated. Pull off heat and allow mix to cool. season with salt and pepper.
  8. Unfold a sheet of puff pastry. Cut 1/3 to reserve for lattice. The larger peice lightly-floured and roll out 13x15-inch rectangle.
  9. place prosciutoo slices slightly overlapping leaving an inch border uncovered. Spread mustard over prosciutto. Spread mushroom dexelles
  10. Center beef loaf onto pastry. Bring in the side pinching edges together. Fold and seal beef loaf completely inside. Brush with egg yolk
  11. Prepare lattice and spread on top of loaf. Brush lattice with egg yolk
  12. Place pasty-wrapped loaf on rack
  13. Bake in oven for 30-45 min, until center reaches at least 160 degrees F. Take out of oven and allow it to rest for 10 min before slicing.
